The History of Baseball

A concise overview from the game's origins to early milestones

Baseball developed from a family of bat-and-ball games that were played in England and then evolved in North America during the 19th century. Over time, it became deeply woven into American culture and later spread internationally.

This page covers early developments and key milestones in baseball's formative years. Click through to Page 2 for the modern era and notable figures.

Early Developments

  • Traditional English bat-and-ball games influenced early versions of baseball.
  • 1846 – the first recorded organized game of baseball in Hoboken, NJ.
  • Mid-1800s – the Knickerbocker Rules helped standardize play.

Key Milestones

  1. 1846 — First recorded organized game (Hoboken, NJ).
  2. 1869 — Cincinnati Red Stockings become the first fully professional team.
  3. 1903 — First modern World Series between AL and NL champions.
