Expansion and Modern Era

During the 20th century, baseball expanded to become America’s national pastime. Teams grew in number, leagues developed rivalries, and the game adapted through world events such as the Great Depression and World War II.

1947

Jackie Robinson broke the color barrier, becoming the first African American player in Major League Baseball.

Other Milestones

1969

Baseball celebrated its 100th anniversary and introduced divisional play.

1994

The World Series was canceled due to a players’ strike, a major disruption in baseball history.

2000s

Baseball entered a new era with expanded international presence and the introduction of advanced analytics.
